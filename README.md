## How to Calculate Pivot Points for Day Trading

### Standard Pivot Point Formula

The most widely used pivot point calculation is the **Classic Pivot Point** method:

```
Pivot Point (PP) = (Previous High + Previous Low + Previous Close) / 3

Resistance 1 (R1) = (2 √ó PP) - Previous Low
Support 1 (S1) = (2 √ó PP) - Previous High

Resistance 2 (R2) = PP + (Previous High - Previous Low)
Support 2 (S2) = PP - (Previous High - Previous Low)

Resistance 3 (R3) = Previous High + 2 √ó (PP - Previous Low)
Support 3 (S3) = Previous Low - 2 √ó (Previous High - PP)
```

### Example Calculation (EUR/USD)

**Previous Day Data:**
- High: 1.1050
- Low: 1.1000
- Close: 1.1030

**Pivot Point Calculation:**
```
PP = (1.1050 + 1.1000 + 1.1030) / 3 = 1.1027

R1 = (2 √ó 1.1027) - 1.1000 = 1.1054
S1 = (2 √ó 1.1027) - 1.1050 = 1.1004

R2 = 1.1027 + (1.1050 - 1.1000) = 1.1077
S2 = 1.1027 - (1.1050 - 1.1000) = 1.0977
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid in Pivot Point Scalping

### ‚ùå Mistake #1: Trading Against the Trend

**Problem:** Trying to scalp SHORT at R1 when the daily trend is strongly bullish.

**Solution:** Add 20 EMA or 50 EMA to identify the dominant trend. Only take bounce trades WITH the trend, and breakout trades when breaking key levels.

### ‚ùå Mistake #2: Ignoring Spread Costs

**Problem:** Spread costs can consume a significant portion of small scalping moves, especially on exotic pairs.

**Solution:** 
- Stick to major pairs (EUR/USD spread: 0.5-1 pip)
- Avoid exotic pairs (spread: 5-20 pips)
- Trade during high liquidity sessions
- Use ECN accounts for tightest spreads

### ‚ùå Mistake #3: Overtrading

**Problem:** Taking every pivot touch leads to poor trade selection and emotional exhaustion.

**Solution:**
- Set maximum daily trade limit (5-10 trades)
- Wait for confirmation (rejection candle + RSI)
- Skip marginal setups near news events
- Quality > Quantity

### ‚ùå Mistake #4: Not Accounting for News Events

**Problem:** Pivot levels become unreliable during high-impact news (NFP, FOMC, CPI).

**Solution:**
- Check economic calendar daily
- Avoid trading 30 minutes before/after major news
- If holding position during news, move stop to breakeven
- Resume normal scalping 1 hour after release

### ‚ùå Mistake #5: Using Fixed Position Sizes

**Problem:** Risking the same dollar amount on 5-pip stops and 20-pip stops.

**Solution:** Use proper position sizing:
```
Position Size = (Account Risk √ó Account Size) / Stop Loss in Pips

Example:
- Account: $10,000
- Risk: 1% = $100
- Stop: 10 pips
- Position: $100 / 10 = 1.0 lot
```
